About MUN

1 . MUN is an authentic simulation of the UN conference wherein the participants are referred to as delegates and are given a chance to represent a nation or state and discuss various issues and crises which have an influence on international politics.
2 . MUN inculcates the qualities of research, public speaking, diplomacy, debating, writing skills and leadership.
3 . Various committees such as UNSC, WHO, UNICEF etc. are setup to give immense exposure to the delegates.

UNSC



Vikram Advani
Chair

One of the principal organs of the UN, its main focus is to maintain International peace and security. It makes changes in the UN charter, appoints new members in the council, and along with the General Assembly (GA) elects judges of the International Court of Judges (ICJ). It consists of total 15 members; 5 Permanent and 10 Non-Permanent and the rest observers, each member has 1 vote. It calls upon the members to the dispute and makes attempts to solve it peacefully.

UNHRC



Avipsita Das
Chair

A UN body which deals protection and promotion of human rights, it was established by UN General Assembly on March 25, 2006. UNHRC has 47 members elected for a term of 3 years on a regional basis: 13 for Africa, 13 for Asia, 6 for Eastern Europe, 8 for Latin America and the Caribbean (GRULAC), and 7 for the Western European and Others Group (WEOG).

LOKSABHA



Swaima Ahmad
Speaker

The Lok Sabha (House of the People) is the lower house of India's bicameral Parliament. Members of the Lok Sabha are elected by adult universal suffrage and a first-past-the-post system to represent their respective constituencies, and they hold their seats for five years or until the body is dissolved by the President on the advice of the council of ministers. The house meets in the Lok Sabha Chambers of the Sansad Bhavan in New Delhi. The maximum strength of the house is 552. In TechMUN the main motive of Loksabha is to discuss and implement the improving developments in India.

FAQ


What is MUN (Model United Nations)?

Model UN or MUN is basically an academic simulation of of the United Nations that aims to encourage students to discuss about the major issues concerning the world and international politics.

What is an Agenda?

Agenda refers to the particular issue on which the committee would be discussing. It is the primary reason for assembling.

Who can register for techNITi19 MUN?

Participation in techNITi19 MUN is open to working professionals and students pursuing their undergraduate, post graduate and PhD. School students cannot participate.

What is the registration process for techNITi19 MUN?

A form is available in the website when you tap on the registration tab. Fill your preferences accordingly so as to get your allotments.

When will the allotments be released?

It would be released at the end of January month when the registrations are closed.

What are the dates of the conference?

techNITi19 MUN conference will be conducted on 16th & 17th Feb 2019. It is a two day conference.

How the double delegation process is done?

By filling the techNITi19 ID of Co-delegate in the registration form

What are the modes of payment?

There are two modes of payment, offline at the techNITi19 reception desk in VNIT and online. For any queries feel free to contact us at the number mentioned in the website.

When will the details regarding the accommodation would be given?

We’ll be sending a mail regarding all the details once you fill the google form.

What is the best way to reach the venue?

The venue of the conference is Visvesvaraya National Institute of Technology (VNIT), Nagpur. Various means of public and private transport are available from the railway station as well as airport.

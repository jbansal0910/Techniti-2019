AQUAHUNT
REGISTER

About

With an ever-increasing demand for unthinkable technology, are you up to the challenge to hybridize a rescue ship with the touch of electronic machinery? Enter Aquahunt and we’ll find out.

Problem Statement

The event includes building a boat, having mounted on it a mechanism for lifting and dropping objects and carrying them from one place to another in a water tank. The mechanism can be hydraulic, mechanical or electrical and any new technology is also welcome. The boat dimensions must satisfy the given conditions.

Gameplay

STAGE 1:

It consists of teams picking up as many number of painted blocks as they could with their aquabot with each block carrying points and transferring them to the safe island and in the way they have to pass through the turbulent region. Any block falling in water will result in negative points.
Top four high scoring teams will pass on to the second stage.

STAGE 2:

It consists of faceoff between two bots of respective teams. They have to take the blocks from the pickup point to the destination point. The team with most number of blocks will win. No team is allowed to harm the other team’s bot. Two of such matches will take place and the two winners will play the final stage.

STAGE 3:

The two teams will compete in same manner as in round 2 but with turbulent region being increased.

Rules and Regulations

GENERAL RULES

  • Every participant must register at www.axisvnit.org.
  • The robot should fit in a box of 30 x 30 x 30 cm in its most compact form.
  • A Maximum of 4 people can be there in a group.
  • The robot has to be necessarily controlled by some wired/wireless remote controlled mechanism at all stages of the event.
  • The controlling wires should not be held taut at any point of the event.
  • Wireless bot will be awarded extra points in preliminary round i.e. the ‘First Mission’.
  • Use of Lego kits or readymade mechanisms is strictly prohibited. Violation of this clause will lead to disqualification of the robot. However, readymade gear and shafts may be used.
  • The potential difference between any two points on the bot should not exceed 12V.
  • A 12V power supply will be provided by us.
  • Damage to the arena by the bot may result in disqualification.


    • POINTS SYSTEM

  • The points system described below are for the ‘First Round’ only.
  • The points for the Second Round will be disclosed at the time of ‘Second Round’ itself, although no modifications in the design of bots is required for the ‘Second Round’.
  • Each team will be given 10 minutes to complete the tasks/event. For testing of their bots, 2 extra minutes will be given before the start of tasks/event.
  • The points are decided according to placement of the blocks on the safety islands ranging from 10 to 30.
  • Every time, the bot passes the turbulence region, extra 5 points will be awarded.
  • If the team drops any block into the water, 5 points will be deducted from their total score.
  • Grabbing and placing the T-shaped block( Thor’s Hammer) successfully on any of the safety islands will be awarded with extra 100 points.
  • Innovation in the design of bots will be awarded with 50 points.


    • NOTE :

    In case of any dispute, techNITi19 Executive Committee’s decision will be final and binding.

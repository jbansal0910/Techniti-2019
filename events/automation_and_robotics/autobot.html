Problem Statement

Teams have to built an Autonomous bot which will follow a black line and keep track of directions. The bot have to analyse the path in dry run and solve the maze in the shortest path in the final run. The event includes two rounds. All the contestants participating will have to participate for the first round. Thus the first round serves to be qualifying round to participate in the second round.

Bots coded as Finite State Machines are encouraged for this year’s theme of FSM.

Gameplay

  • SIMPLE LINE FOLLOWER


    • ARENA DESCRIPTION
  • The base of the arena is made up of white colored flex sheet with a black colored line {to be followed} on it.
  • The thickness of the line will be 3 cm.
  • The course line may have acute, obtuse and right angles, curves.
  • Organizers’ decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.


    • ROBOT SPECIFICATION
  • The robot should fit inside a cube of side 25 cm in all directions.
  • Robot should be completely autonomous.
  • The bot can be both internally or externally powered.
  • The power supply may or not be on board. Potential at any point on the robot should not exceed 20 V.
  • A tolerance of 5% is allowed in the dimension



  • MAZE FOLLOWER AND SOLVER


    • ARENA DESCRIPTION
  • The base of the arena is made up of white coloured flex sheet with a black coloured line{to be followed} on it.
  • The thickness of the line will be 3 cm.
  • The course line may have acute, obtuse and right angles, curves.
  • It will be complete maze having one or multiple solutions to solve it.
  • Organizers’ decision shall be treated as final and binding on all.


    • ROBOT SPECIFICATION
  • The robot should fit inside a cube of side 25 cm in all directions.
  • Robot should be completely autonomous.
  • The bot can be both internally or externally powered.
  • The power supply may or not be on board. Potential at any point on the robot should not exceed 20V.
  • A tolerance of 5% is allowed in the dimensions.

    • Rules and Regulations

  • All the students enrolled in high school, undergraduate, postgraduate (excluding PhD.) program at any recognized institute (identity card will be checked) are eligible to participate.
  • A team can consist of a maximum of 4 members.
  • Each team must declare a name for their machine at the time of competition.


    • General Rules

  • Every participant must register at www.axisvnit.org.
  • The robot is expected to follow the line accurately. If the robot deviates from the line, contestants are allowed to keep the bot manually in its right direction{hand touch}
  • ONLY 2 HAND TOUCHES are allowed, each rendering -10 [negative] points.
  • 1 restart is allowed, but would cost -30 points.
  • A sample arena will be available for calibration and practice, final arena would be disclosed only after start of the rounds
  • All the robots will have to be submitted to the organizers before start ;and no changes would be allowed to be made to robots afterwards.
  • Points on FSM will be decided only in case of cut throat Competition.
  • Between trials and event, participants should not feed information about the arena to the bot. However, participants are allowed to: Adjust sensors (Gain, Position etc.), Change speed settings and Make repairs. The judges shall arbitrate.
  • After successfully completing the maze “TIME” required will be the Evaluation parameter.
  • Event Managers & Coordinators reserve the right to ask for the explanation of the robot.{Programming, circuit, etc.}
  • Winner of the competition will be one who will solve the maze completely. The time based evaluation may be done if there are many teams solving the maze effectively and in shortest path.
  • Participants are not allowed to keep anything inside the arena other than the bot.
  • In case of any disputes / discrepancies, the organizers’ decision will be final and binding.
  • The participants are requested not to assume anything without discussing with the event managers.
  • The organizers reserve the rights to change any or all of the above rules as they deem fit.
  • Innovative ideas are welcome and separate awards may be given for best design of the bot, best bot and many more.
  • The competition would be held during techNITi19. Venue and time would be available near the auditorium.
  • Any changes in rules, structure would be uploaded to the website.

    • NOTE :In case of any dispute, techNITi19 Executive Committee’s decision will be final and binding.

    Participation

  • All the students enrolled in high school, undergraduate, postgraduate (excluding PhD.) program at any recognized institute (identity card will be checked) are eligible to participate.
  • A team can consist of a maximum of 4 members.
  • Each team must declare a name for their machine at the time of competition.

    • Disqualification

    Any damage to arena, failure to abide by rules, tampering and impolite behaviour would lead to disqualification. Organisers hold rights to disqualify any team if they feel that the team is not playing with fair interests.

About

Ever watched the movie Robot and day dreamt of creating one? Don’t tell us you haven’t. Well this is your chance to prove your mechanical mettle (and maybe a step closer to achieving your dream) by creating a robot capable of knocking everyone’s socks off.

Problem Statement

A manually controlled robot is to be built that can create structures, align blocks and tackle and handle the obstacles in the way you command it to. Amaze the crowd and spectators with your knowledge of creating an artificial and mechanical imitator.

Game Play

The game is divided into two rounds:
1st Round:
  • The bot has to pick and place the block of certain dimension in specified spot by carrying it through provided passage.
  • Then it has to pick and place the next block of same dimension in the next spot by passing through the wedge arrangement.
  • Next to the wedge arrangement is another block placed prior to the zig-zag path which has to be knocked off from the rotating disk.
  • Another block has to be carried across the adjustable rail and a single step arrangement and has to be released in the given spot.
    2nd Round:
  • The shortlisted teams from the 1st round will be participating in this round. Short listing will be done on the basis of point scored by each team and time taken to complete the first round. Block lifting is completely excluded in this round.
  • First the bot has to cross the banking path of certain radius of curvature without falling off.
  • Next, the bot has to cross see-saw terrain leading to the bridge containing holes and pits arranged on the wedge.
  • Next stage consists of wavy path, following it has a small cylinder which has to be carried across the net bridge and place it vertically.

    • Rules and Regulations

    General Rules:
  • The participants should register at www.axisvnit.org.
  • The maximum no. of members in a team is 5.
  • The members of a team can be from any institution.
  • All the members of a team should have valid college ID cards with them.
  • Any participant using vulgar languages during the game play may result to the disqualification of the team.
  • No participant can be the member of more than one team and no teams can share their bots.
  • The event managers are free to modify the rules under any circumstances. The teams will be duly notified any changes occurring when required.
  • NOTE: In case of any dispute, techNITi19 Executive Committee’s decision will be final and binding.
    Technical Details:
  • Only the event organizers are allowed to touch the arena. Participants are strictly restricted from touching the arena during the game play.
  • Bots damaging the arena may lead to direct disqualification of the team.
  • The organizing team will not be responsible for any damages to the bot during the game play.
  • Once the game started, no modification to the bot is allowed.
  • Time measured by the organizing team is only considered. No concern is given to the time measured by the participants.
  • The wires of the bot should be handled very carefully by the team members themselves.

    • Robot specification

  • The dimensions of the bot should not exceed 30cm*30cm*25cm (l*b*h).
  • The speed of the motors used for locomotion (i.e. for wheels) should not exceed 300 RPM.
  • Any mechanism for grabbing the block is allowed only with the bot.
  • The power supply for the bot should not exceed 12V and 2 Amp.
  • The teams can use their own power supply or the power supply provided by the organizing team.
  • The power supply should be placed outside the arena.

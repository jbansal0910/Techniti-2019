About

In an age where human ability and athleticism goes beyond anything seen before (and France is the undisputed FIFA Champion) we at techNITi19’19 are searching for bots which can compete with the best in the world. Our mission is to make robots that can not only compete with but surpass human ability by using state of the art mechanisms to prove that robots are unbeatable in their footballing skills.
Escalating to a stage of robot-oriented football, if you have the enthusiasm and passion to contribute a little to this mission, design a manually controlled mechanical bot which can kick or push a ball into the opponent’s goal post.
The next Messi will not be born, but built.

Problem Statement

Design a manually controlled mechanical bot which can kick or push a ball (a medium sized plastic ball) into the opponent’s goal post. The main motive of the game is to kick a ball into the opponent’s goal post and prevent the ball from going into yours.

Game Play

The game is divided into two rounds:
1st Round:
It is a time based round where the bot has to complete the given tasks in minimum time. The task is to kick/push the balls which are placed in different locations of the arena into a single goal post by passing through the obstacles (acting as defenders). The bot completing the task in minimum time will be qualified for the next round.
2nd Round:
The shortlisted teams from the 1st round will be participating in this round. There will be one to one match for the knock out. The teams clearing knock out will then again be shortlisted and divided into Groups.1 team will be selected from each group which will then proceed for the further phase.
Note:
  • Once the one-on-one match system proceeds, there will be goal keeper placed on the goal posts. The goal keeper will be the part of arena.
  • For the semi-final and final rounds, the lifted goal post will again be placed on the original level (i.e. on the ground surface). But in this case, the goal post will be divided into 2 parts i.e. upper goal post and lower goal post. Scoring goals on the upper goal post provides double points. Ball striking the partition line will be considered as lower goal.
  • There will also be the provisions of penalty, free-kick and throw which are discussed in the “Rules” section below. The points may vary for these conditions.

    • Rules and Regulations

    General Rules (Basic requirements for participation)
  • The participants should register at www.axisvnit.org.
  • The maximum no. of members in a team is 5.
  • The members of a team can be from any institution.
  • All the members of a team should have valid college ID cards with them.
  • Any participant using vulgar languages during the game play may result to the disqualification of the team.
  • No participant can be the member of more than one team and no teams can share their bots.
  • The event managers are free to modify the rules under any circumstances. The teams will be duly notified any changes occurring when required. The decision of the event managers will be the final!
    Technical Details:
  • Only the event organizers are allowed to touch the arena. Participants are strictly restricted from touching the arena during the game play.
  • Bots damaging the arena may lead to direct disqualification of the team.
  • The organizing team will not be responsible for any damages to the bot during the game play.
  • Once the game started, no modification to the bot is allowed.
  • Time measured by the organizing team is only considered. No concern is given to the time measured by the participants.
  • During the game, the decisions will be made by the referee on the spot and they will be final and binding.
  • Referee is free to give penalty in case of any violence.
  • The wires of the bot should be handled very carefully by the team members themselves.
  • Bot damaging the opponent’s bot will immediately be disqualified.
    Game Overview:
  • The ball used in the game will be a medium sized plastic/polymer ball (almost equal to the size of golf ball).
  • The side for the bot will be decided by toss. However, the side will be changed after half-time.
  • The game will start from the center. The ball will be placed at center and the bots will be placed at their respective goal lines (the rear wheels of the bot touching the goal line).
  • In case a bot kicks the ball out of the arena, the ball will be provided to the opponent bot.
  • Ball start should always be a kick or push. A bot cannot drag the ball during the start or free kick (in any case).
  • A bot cannot keep or shield the ball for more than 10 seconds. Any bot found doing such thing will be taken as foul and free kick is given to opponent bot.
  • In case of any free-kicks or fouls, the ball will be started from the starting point.
  • The bot has full freedom to move within the arena except inside the goal line.
  • In case of any situations where no team is responsible for the mistake, organizers decision will be final.
    Robot specification:
  • The dimensions of the bot should not exceed 30cm*30cm*25cm (l*b*h).
  • The speed of the motors used for locomotion (i.e. for wheels) should not exceed 300 RPM.
  • Any mechanism for kicking the ball is allowed except blowing. (A bot cannot consist of a fan for blowing the ball.)
  • Additionally, the bot is expected to take small aerial shots. However, this is not mandatory.
  • The power supply for the bot should not exceed 12V and 2 Amp.
  • The teams can use their own power supply or the power supply provided by the organizing team.
  • The power supply should be placed outside the arena.

    • FAQs

    Q. Is there any registration fee specifically for this event?
    Ans. No, there isn’t any registration charge for this event.
    Q. Is it necessary to register for this event online or can we register on the spot?
    Ans. You are guaranteed to participate if you register online on time. However, in any case, if you are unable to do so, you can register on the registration desk in VNIT campus before the event.
    Q. Can we control our robot wireless?
    Ans. Yes, of course! We encourage innovative ideas and we do have provision of extra points for wireless robots.
    Q. What will be the time span of the event?
    Ans. Event is spread across 2 days within the techNITi19’15 time frame.
    Q. I am a beginner in robotics. Can I still participate?
    Ans. Of course. We encourage beginners to participate by conducting workshops. However, if you are not able to attend the workshops, tutorial videos will be provided for the same.
    Q. Is there any limit for the speed of motors?
    Ans. Yes, the motors used for locomotion (i.e. for wheels) should not exceed 300 RPM. However, any other motors used for any other purposes do not have any limits.

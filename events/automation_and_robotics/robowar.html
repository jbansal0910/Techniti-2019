About

Everyone has watched and enjoyed Transformers since their childhood. Autobots using their advanced weapons to protect the Earth from Decepticons or Hugh Jackman punching through Robots in Real Steel. Ever wondered how a battle between two such extremely powerful futuristic robots would look like? The stage is set for one of the most brutal and electrifying events of techNITi19’19. techNITi19’19 brings you one of the largest battle arenas in central India and the best robots coming to battle it out to decide who takes the glory. It’s not the survival of the strongest. It’s the survival of the smartest! As simple as it can get. Win or die. Fiercely competitive by all the teams from the country. Steel cutting steel, metal gnawing metal! Be a part of the largest audience for Robowars on the campus. Get ready for the ultimate showdown!

Problem Statement

Design and fabricate a wired or wireless manually controlled robot capable of winning in the arena. The main objective should be to destroy or immobilize the opponent bot! (As you like it)

Rules and Regulations

  • The maximum number of participants allowed per team is 6.
  • During the match only 2 members of the team will be allowed to operate the bot.
  • Wireless Robot should ensure zero interference.
  • Do not use any tether cables, projectiles, nets.
  • No chemical, inflammable or incendiary materials.
  • No liquids are allowed.
  • Robots should not employ any RF jammers or any other similar equipment.
  • If any robot tries to or damages the arena the robot will be disqualified.
  • If the Cage gets damaged during the prefinal rounds and the cage is not liable to play for the next round,the winner and the prize money won't be declared
  • Team Name: Every team must have a unique name. techNITi19 reserves the right to reject entries from any team whose name it deems inappropriate, offensive or conflicting. Managers must be notified if a team's name has been changed.
  • Team Representative: Each team must specify their Team Representative (Leader) at the time of registration on the website. All the important communications between techNITi19 and the registered teams will be done through their Team Representative. The Team representative must submit valid contact details (phone no., email ID etc.) at the time of registration.

    • Robot specification

  • Max permissible dimensions: 75x75x85 cm at the starting of the match.
  • Max permissible weight of the robot: 60 Kg (including power supply).
  • Cylinders for Pneumatics are excluded from the weight of the robot, if and only if they are kept outside the arena at all times. Such robots are not eligible for wireless bonus. Wireless robots will have an advantage in case of a tie.
  • Use of Pneumatics is permitted.
  • Any arrangement made only for hoisting the cables is not counted in maximum height limit.
  • Participants must provide means(nozzle) to measure the cylinder pressure.
  • All bots should have easily visible and controlled mobility in order to compete.
  • ‘COMPRESSOR’ in any case will not be allowed.

    • Safety Instructions

    Teams using pneumatics or hydraulics will have to send a safety letter containing targeted maximum pressure and type of equipment to be used, signed by any faculty in-charge/Lab in- charge or a testing lab/company dealing in this field, on their official letterhead only.

    Match Duration and Type

    Matches shall be 3 minutes of active fight time exclusive of any time-outs. Hence, it is not binding but advisable to keep battery capacity, power usage and machine defenses such that they can sustain a 3 minutes fight.

    Gaming Criteria

  • A robot is declared victorious if its opponent is immobilized.
  • A robot will be declared immobile if it cannot display linear motion of at least one inch in a timed period of 10 seconds. A bot with one side of its drivetrain disabled will not be counted out if it can demonstrate some degree of controlled movement. In case both the robots remain mobile after the end of the round then the winner will be decided subjectively.
  • A robot that is deemed unsafe by the judges after the match has begun will be disqualified and therefore, declared the loser. The match will be immediately halted and the opponent will be awarded a win.
  • If a robot is thrown out of the arena the match will be stopped immediately, and the robot inside the arena will automatically be declared as the winner.
  • Robots cannot win by pinning or lifting their opponents. Organizers will allow pinning or lifting for a maximum of 20 seconds per pin/lift then the attacker robot will be instructed to release the opponent. If, after being instructed to do so, the attacker is able to release but does not, their robot may be disqualified. If two or more robots become entangled or a crushing or gripping weapon is employed and becomes trapped within another robot, then the competitors should make the timekeeper aware, the fight should be stopped and the robots separated by the safest means.
  • Points will be given on the basis of aggression, damage, control and strategy. a. Aggression: Aggression is judged by the frequency, severity, boldness and effectiveness of attacks deliberately initiated by the robot against its opponent. If a robot appears to have accidentally attacked an opponent, that act will not be considered Aggression. b. Control: Control means the ability to attack an opponent at its weakest point, use its Weapons in the most effective way, and minimize the damage caused by the opponent or its weapons. c. Damage: Through deliberate action, a robot either directly or indirectly reduces the functionality, effectiveness or defensibility of an opponent. Damage is not considered relevant if a robot inadvertently harms itself. Also, if a pressure vessel or a rapidly spinning device on a robot fragments, any damage to the opponent will not be considered "deliberate". d. Strategy: The robot exhibits a combat plan that exploits the robot's strengths against the weaknesses of its opponent. Strategy is also defined as a robot exhibiting a deliberate defense plan that guards its weaknesses against the strengths of the opponent.
  • NOTE: Qualification of a robot to next level is subjective and totally on the decision of the judges.

    • Arena Specification

    Safety Precautions: The battle area available to the robots for motion, during the match, shall be 1ft inside the polycarbonate walls, to prevent direct impact. The arena shall be protected by polycarbonate walls of 5mm thickness at four sides (excluding the top). The top shall be protected by 5mm polycarbonate sheet. Arena Diagram: The out-to-out dimension of the arena will be 16ftX16ftX10ft (l x b x h).

    Submission of Abstract

    Participants have to submit an abstract of their machine, consisting of a written abstract and a video of the working model before the competition. The teams can do an online submission, mailing us the soft copy of abstract (PDF format) and a video of your robot at mansit.suman@axisvnit.org with subject Robowars: <Team Name>.

    Abstract

  • The weapon systems and power supply method should be explained in detail, along with proper diagrams. Picture(s) showing these should be attached.
  • Functioning of wireless remote and the frequency or any other wireless module used for wireless remote must be explained in detail.
  • Description of any unusual advantageous mechanism used. The specifications of all the components used, including motors, suspension springs, remote controller, wires, battery etc. have to be mentioned.
  • An email will be sent to the team leader confirming the receipt of the entry. Each team is allowed to make online submission only by email. In case of multiple submissions, only the first submission will be used for judging purposes.
  • Further instructions will be updated on website.

    • Video Abstract


  • The video should be of at least 1 minute with the unedited clip showing the machine performance to the fullest. All destructive mechanism(s) being used must be shown with their working
  • It is not necessary to explain the mechanisms in the video. All videos will be used strictly for seeding purposes. techNITi19 assures total privacy of the matter submitted to us. The video of your machine will be helpful in future as an evidence of your hard-work along with determining your position for the competition. Hence, please pay adequate attention to it. All submission must be made online

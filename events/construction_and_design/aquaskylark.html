About

In this event you are supposed to prepare a water propelled rocket which will be launch by using a launcher which we provide you or you also can have your own launcher. There will be extra points for launcher. About the events basically we apply funda of projectile motion in which we analyse
1. Range
2. Time of flight
3. Deviation
There will be suitable point for each part and the team scores more point will we considered as winner.

Problem Statement

Construct a water propelled rocket that works with air pressure to compete with the following rounds.

Gameplay

ROUND 1:

  • The objective of the preliminary round is to launch a water-rocket without any payload, and make it go as far as possible from the launch point i.e. the maximum horizontal distance.
  • The horizontal distance will be considered from the starting point to the point where any part of the rocket first touches the ground
  • Multi-stage rockets are also allowed but the combined tank capacity must be 2 liters only.
  • Each team shall be allowed two chances to launch and the better of the two will be considered (This may be altered later based on the performance of most teams.)
  • Note: Pressure limit for this round will be 75 psi
  • Only 30 percent of the candidates appearing in first round will be qualifying into second round which depends upon the score they get

    • ROUND 2:

    Round Two tests the accuracy of your rockets.
  • There will going to be various regions with specific angles n participants get point according in which region there rocket launch.
  • Like if your rocket strike first in region of 30 degree you will get 100 pts, in region of 30-60 degree 80 and so on.
  • Only 30 percent of the candidates appearing in second round will be qualifying into third round which depends upon the score they g

    • ROUND 3:

  • In this round the teams whose rocket will remain in air for maximum time will get maximum point. The participants can even launch their rocket according to them.

    • Rules and Regulations

  • A team may consist of a maximum of 3 member.
  • Teams may use different rockets for different rounds.
  • Cross college teams are also allowed.
  • The rocket must be built completely with plastic bottle.
  • The rocket must use tap water as its primary reaction mass. Thrust must come from expelling reaction mass/water, not from air discharge.
  • Participants will be provided with tap water.
  • The angle at which the rocket is to be launched can be decided by the participant.
  • The organizers can provide you a launcher or you can also bring your own launcher.
  • The rocket must use compressed air as its energy source. A normal bicycle pump as well as a foot pump will be provided to compress the air. The size of the pump outlet will be the standard type available in the market. If your design has a different nozzle size, please be sure to bring your own nozzle and connecting pipe.
  • All parts of the rockets should be properly attached to the main body. If any part (fins etc.) breaks away during flight, it will compromise the safety of the spectators. Therefore, such water rockets may be disqualified from further participation at the discretion of the organizers
  • Each team is strongly advised to bring two rockets of the same design (the second acting as a backup) in case of any damage .The organizers will not be responsible for damage suffered by rockets during the competition. Extra time shall not be given for remaking of damaged rockets.

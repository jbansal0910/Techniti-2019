Problem Statement

Design a truss bridge using Popsicle Sticks and Fevicol

Story Line

A rise in demand of coal, oil and other minerals was envisaged by the government of a thriving state. They, after days of discussion opted for the use of railroads to transport the minerals and oils. It was favoured as it would allow to transport greater amount of goods in a less time. Moreover, it avoided roadway congestion. A government contractor was assigned the project to lay down railway tracks to facilitate the transport of minerals and oils to the state. But the railway lines could not be connected as they encountered a river. Engineers decided to connect the railway lines through a truss bridge over the river which would have the strength to bear a heavy freight locomotive. Now, imagine yourself in their position and construct a truss bridge using. Use your inventiveness to make a sturdy and aesthetically pleasing bridge.

Rules and Regulations

  • The structure made (Trussed Bridge) will be tested on the day of event.
  • Popsicle sticks can be altered physically by cutting or notching at any angle.
  • Only Fevicol can be used as adhesive ,use of other adhesive may lead to disqualification.
  • Maximum number of sticks that can be stack together is Three.
  • Base of the bridge should not be made as shown in the figure.
  • Use of any other adhesive material than Fevicol is not allowed.
  • Thickness of base (deck) should not be more than thickness of Three sticks
  • Use of Threads and Nails is not allowed.
  • Bridge dimensions should be within specified limits as given in the figure.
  • Thickness of the base of the bridge should not exceed 2cm.(i.e. thickness equals to three sticks stacked together)
  • Weight of bridge should not exceed the weight of 600 popsicle sticks.
  • Maximum 4 students per team.
  • Any one of the team member will have to appear for the written test after the testing of bridge

    • Testing of Bridge

  • Bridge model will be loaded till failure. The maximum deflection at the point of yielding and The load at that moment will be used to evaluate the structure.
  • If it happens that the structure reaches the threshold deflection of 1.0 cm then the evaluation will be made on the same load. (At this case the structure will not be tested further.)
  • Even if a single member of structure fails then evaluation will be made on the same load.

    • Judging And Scoring

  • First the structure will be reviewed to check if it violates any rules mentioned above.
  • The bridge will be scored on how well the material has been used to support the load.
  • The efficiency will be calculated as the ratio of ultimate load capacity and the bridge weight.
  • The deflection of the bridge at the yielding will be noted. If the deflection increases more than 10 mm ,then the load at the deflection will be taken as ultimate load.
  • Violating any of the conditions mentioned above ,penalty will be imposed or may lead to disqualification.
  • Weight exceeds the limit - Penalty of 20% of total score
  • Dimensional specifications are not met - Penalty of 10% of total score
  • Use of materials ,except the one stated in rules penalty of 50% of total score or can lead to disqualification

    • Written Test

  • Any one member of the team has to appear for the written test succeded by the testing of the structure
  • The written test comprises of the questions on the basic Engineering Mechanics.
  • The total score of the team is calculated from the testing of the structure and the marks scored in the written test.
  • Written test is mandatory for all the teams participating in the event, if a team misses the written test, it may lead to direct disqualification.
  • Participants should carry the stationary material such as pen, pencil, etc.

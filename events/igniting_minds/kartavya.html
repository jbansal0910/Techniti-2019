Kartavya is a socio-technical event of techNITi19 whose main motive is to provide a technical solution to the problems faced by the elite group NGOs as the to-be engineers. The event is one such platform to creative social awareness in the minds of the students to uplift the needy and promote social welfare. If you seek some peace of mind, this is a great chance to give back to the society.
The participants are asked to draft a feasible solution to the major problem of an NGO chosen thus creating a bridge between NGOs and engineers.

Problem Statement

“If you are 'Fantastic' then do something Drastic to cut the 'Plastic' ”

Nothing exemplifies modernity like plastic. It’s cheap. It can be molded into all sorts of shapes and textures, dyed any color or be transparent. Unlike glass or ceramics, it’s flexible and doesn’t easily break, and it won’t rot or corrode like wood or metal.


Ok, so far plastic sounds pretty great-and it is. The new waterproof 5rs notes are neat and who doesn’t like using glitter.


In India, 70 percent of total plastic consumption is discarded as waste. Around 5.6 million tonnes per annum (TPA) of plastic waste is generated in country, which is about 15,342 tonnes per day (TPD).


Due to lack of knowledge of disposing and inefficient management of the plastic in villages, people trash plastic in the way they want. plastics are seen alongside roads, gutters etc. Also, improper disposal of plastic causes soil degradation. Cows that eat plastic, and do not eat healthy food, are not able to give milk. They die quickly as they are undernourished and vulnerable to diseases. Since the cities are normally far from the villages, it is not possible for the villagers to regularly collect and transport the plastics for the recycling.


Give a technical and economically feasible solution and make a prototype (working or non-working) which can be easily implemented in villages.


EXTRA POINTS WILL BE AWARDED FOR A WORKING PROTOTYPE.


(For example:
Plastic Waste as Road Construction Material:
This process comprises of four major steps; Segregation, Cleaning, Shredding and Collection.Plastic waste with the maximum thickness of 60 microns is collected and then cleaned followed by drying. Dried waste is shredded and added to the heated aggregate in the equal amounts. Right off the bat hot Bitumen has to be added to the plastic and aggregate mixture to use on the road.)

Game Play

The event would consist of two rounds:

ROUND 1:

In this round, the participants have to submit an abstract based on the solution for problem statement given. These would be problems faced by NGOs. This problem statement has to be answered in 150-200 words. The solution should be practical and applicable in real life scenario. Abstract should be uploaded to the link kartavya@axisvnit.org and then participants would be short listed for the next round.

ROUND 2:
You have to present a prototype of the solution suggested (a working or a non-working model) on the day of round 2 along with a presentation which would be presented in front of the panel of judges belonging to the NGO. The winner would be selected based on the points scored. Note: Shortlisted people who are from outside Nagpur, can make a video of their presentation and mail it to us by the intended date which will be informed after shortlisting.
